Overview of Extinct Aerial Animals

Many aerial species, including birds and flying mammals, have become extinct due to hunting, habitat destruction, and climate change. Loss of these species disrupts ecosystems and reduces biodiversity, impacting the balance of nature.

Why Are Aerial Animals Going Extinct?

Human activities such as deforestation, pollution, and hunting have led to the extinction of many species. These activities not only destroy natural habitats but also disrupt the food chain, making it difficult for aerial animals to survive.

Did You Know?

  • The Passenger Pigeon, once numbering in the billions, became extinct in 1914 due to overhunting and habitat loss.
  • The Dodo, native to Mauritius, went extinct in the 17th century after humans and introduced species overexploited the population.
  • Many species of parrots are at risk due to illegal wildlife trade and deforestation.
